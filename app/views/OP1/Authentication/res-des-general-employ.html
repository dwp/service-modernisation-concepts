General

This page explains general research and design comments for the Change employment status journey, which was designed in conjunction with, and is very similar to, the Change bank details journey. As the two journeys are so similar, were tested together and the research analysis is so similar, we have provided general comments only for this journey rather than page-by-page analysis.

Research questions

Decided against reanalysing the data as it is very likely that this journey already exists elsewhere and are not specific to multiple benefits. If this is not the case yet, it should be worked with wireframes that were already tested and used – for instance in Universal Credit or other benefits where people change their employment status.

Did we test it?

This page was tested with 10 users in round 3 of research.

Design changes from the tested version visible in this prototype

The screens in this prototype are very similar to the Mural screens they were recreated from. There are some minor differences, detailed below.

  • Minor changes in font size to make the content hierarchy clearer.
  • Minor changes to bring content into line with the GDS style guide (e.g. capitalisation).
  • Top horizontal navigation header uses the new GDS Design System grey service navigation component (opens in new tab), which is a new component published in summer 2024 after the research took place instead of the header used in the Mural designs, which is not part of the Design System.

General limitations

  • None of the change of circumstances journeys tested were specific to a journey with multiple benefits, nor did they address particular challenges that could arise when combining multiple benefits in a change of circumstances flow.

Next steps

  • Research should focus on journeys that incorporate criteria from multiple benefit lines and realistic scenarios for how multiple benefit payments could change as a consequence of reporting a change.
