General

This page explains research and design that was not specific to a particular page in the change bank details prototype.

What was the design intention?

If users are using a single application form to apply to multiple benefits, it makes logical sense for them to also tell us once about any change of circumstances. It is a pain point for users, having to tell us the same information multiples times, in different ways.

Research questions

  • What research around change of circumstances has been done previously at DWP? (not part of this prototype)
  • What was participants recent experience of updating their circumstances with DWP?
  • What are participants' thoughts on the change of circumstances screens?
  • How would participants update their bank details and can they understand the given change of circumstance categories.

Design changes from the tested version visible in this prototype

The screens in this prototype are very similar to the Mural screens they were recreated from. There are some minor differences, detailed below.

  • Minor changes in font size to make the content hierarchy clearer.
  • Minor changes to bring content into line with the GDS style guide (e.g. capitalisation).
  • Top horizontal navigation header uses the new GDS Design System grey service navigation component (opens in new tab), which is a new component published in summer 2024 after the research took place instead of the header used in the Mural designs, which is not part of the Design System.

General limitations

Design

  • The design system was not followed in all places. Designs were originally created (and tested) as wireframes in Mural and have since been recreated in the prototype kit.
  • The design doesn't explore the relationship with OneLogin or how a change of circumstance might happen if that piece of data is held by OneLogin as well as by a DWP service.

Research

  • The journey tested started with the 'Profile page' so no comprehensive journey was tested.
  • None of the change of circumstances journeys tested were specific to a journey with multiple benefits, nor did they address particular challenges that could arise when combining multiple benefits in a change of circumstances flow.

Next steps

Future design and research should:

  • Include desk research to identify which change of circumstances designs already exist for single benefits and what the UR analysis for these products has revealed.
  • Focus more on the implications of changing circumstances for multiple benefits such as the impact of changing circumstances on other benefits (and how to communicate this to the customer);
  • Examine the entire journey leading up to the change of circumstances page.
  • Find out if users understand the term 'change of circumstances' and what alternative ways could be used to describe it. Universal Credit for instance is working with a different terminology.
  • Base the design of all pages including question pages, confirmation pages, support pages and multi-factor authentication pages on standard design system patterns, at least in the first instance.
  • Look to other government and DWP services for good examples to follow where less common design patterns or challenges arise.
  • Ensure that repeated parts of a design (for example, details for how to contact DWP) are positioned consistently so that users know where to find them.
  • Explore the relationship with OneLogin, including how a user can report a change to a piece of data that is held by OneLogin but also appears in DWP services.
