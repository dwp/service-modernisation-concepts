Support page

This page explains research and design specific to the Support page.

What was the design intention?

  • The intention behind this concept was to explore how to provide support information for users inside the account.
  • Support areas included help with an application as well as exceptional circumstances or signposting to third party support services.
  • We explored how support could be multi-channel, including web chat, phone call, face to face and phone directory.

Did we test it?

This page was tested with 10 participants as part of another journey (change of circumstances) in round 3.

Design changes from the tested version visible in this prototype

  • Moved 'Talk to an advisor' section for consistency with other screens.
  • Minor changes to bring content into line with the GDS style guide (e.g. capitalisation).
  • Top horizontal navigation header uses the new GDS Design System grey service navigation component (opens in new tab), which is a new component published in summer 2024, after the research took place instead of the header used in the Mural designs, which is not part of the Design System.

Detailed research summary

  • The high number of options to contact DWP was generally appreciated by participants.
  • Support via webchat was frequently mentioned as the most convenient method of contact, as it was considered quick and easy.
    • While most users accepted needing to answer a few questions from a bot in order to be redirected to the right department, there was an overall expectation to eventually chat with a real human. Most likely because there was little trust in the ability of a bot to solve problems.
    • Some participants thought that webchat would lead to quicker results than a phone call.
    • One participant said that their preferred choice of channel would depend on the specific problem they would approach DWP with.

Considerations for further research and design work

  • Understand what kind of support channels can actually be provided by DWP.
  • Understand the expectations users have about accessing support.
  • Test the language users expect in relation to accessing support.
  • Explore where in the account users expect to go to for support.
