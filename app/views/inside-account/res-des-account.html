Account home page

This page explains research and design specific to the Account home page.

What was the design intention?

We wanted to concept test the features and functionality of the application tracking, checking payments and providing an update (change of circumstances) parts of the end-to-end journey from the future state service blueprint (opens in new tab). They needed a context for us to explore them so we wireframed what a customer might see once they've logged in.

Did we test it?

This page was tested with around 18 users in round 2 and round 3 of research.

Design changes from the tested version visible in this prototype

  • Minor changes in font size to make the content hierarchy clearer.
  • Minor changes to bring content into line with the GDS style guide (e.g. capitalisation).
  • Top horizontal navigation header uses the new GDS Design System grey service navigation component (opens in new tab), which is a new component published in summer 2024, after the research took place instead of the header used in the Mural designs, which is not part of the Design System.

Detailed research summary

Participants found the page straightforward and concise in terms of both content and design.

  • This new version was preferred by some users familiar with the current application process.
    • One participant liked that the page felt personalised because of the name that was displayed.
  • The 'Your Application' section was the most frequently mentioned section and generally well-liked.
    • Participants appreciated its ability to show where they were in the application process (timeline displayed).
    • Pain points:
      • Some users didn’t understand whether the widget would refer to one or multiple benefits.
      • One person thought the application status would appear at the top of the page.
    • The 'Upcoming Payments' section was mentioned frequently as well.
      • This section helped some participants understand what payments to expect and to be able 'to budget'.
      • Participants usually use the 'View your benefit payments' link or the menu bar to check payments.
    • General pain points:
      • Confusion about why payments take 7 days to appear.
      • Font design concerns (see 'Account and Home Screen' area of Mural board of consolidated analysis, opens in new tab).
      • Missing option to apply for other benefits.
      • Missing log in/log out option.

    Considerations for further research and design work

    • Explore how we can allow a user to track multiple applications and how we can create a consistent experience across pages and benefits. Consider user needs for wanting to see where they are in the application process.
    • Ensure any design for progress tracking meets design guidelines.
    • Understand what communication users currently receive in relation to their benefit payments (letters, text messages, emails and so on).
    • Review the pain points and address them when designing similar pages.
